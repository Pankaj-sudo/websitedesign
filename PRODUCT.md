# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Filipino small business owners — sari-sari stores, boutiques, restaurants, beauty salons, clinics, and home-based sellers — who want an online presence but have no technical background. They browse on mobile, pay via GCash, and decide based on trust and perceived professionalism, not feature checklists. They're price-sensitive but willing to invest if the service feels credible.

## Product Purpose

A sales landing page for a Philippine web design service offering three fixed-price tiers (₱5,000 / ₱12,000 / ₱25,000). The page must build trust fast, explain what each tier includes in plain terms, and convert a visitor into an inquiry or order. The page IS the portfolio: if the page looks bad, no one buys a website.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy · Affordable · Professional

Voice is confident and direct — not startup-hype, not corporate cold. Like a skilled local craftsman who shows you the work and gives you a straight price. Warm enough for the Filipino market; polished enough to charge ₱25,000 without apology.

## Anti-references

- Generic freelancer templates: blue-and-white Canva/Wix-style pages every web designer uses — forgettable, interchangeable, zero trust signal
- SaaS landing pages: too cold, too Silicon Valley, not relatable to a sari-sari store owner in QC
- OLX / Shopee marketplace UI: too busy, too discount-coded, undermines the premium positioning

## Design Principles

1. **The page is the pitch.** The design itself is the primary proof of skill. A mediocre-looking page for a web design service is a self-defeating sale.
2. **Trust before features.** Filipino buyers buy from people they trust. Establish credibility (social proof, clear process, real contact) before listing specs.
3. **Transparent pricing, not a maze.** One-time prices, stated plainly. No asterisks, no "contact for quote." Confidence in the price is part of the brand.
4. **Local context, not a translation.** GCash, Messenger, Viber, Shopee references aren't add-ons; they're the native context this buyer lives in.
5. **Show at every tier.** The sample mockups are the most persuasive element on the page. They must feel distinct and real, not placeholder-y.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Mobile-first — most visitors are on Android mid-range phones. Reduced motion must be respected. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable with visible focus states. No text smaller than 14px on mobile.
